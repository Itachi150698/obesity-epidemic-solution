Liver Disease Prediction

Before we explore the features for predicting liver disease, let's understand how these features contribute to assessing the risk of liver-related issues. Each feature provides valuable insights that aid in evaluating the overall condition of the liver.

  • Total Bilirubin: The total amount of bilirubin in the blood. Ranges from 0 to 3 mg/dL. Elevated values may indicate potential liver dysfunction.
  • Direct Bilirubin: The direct (conjugated) bilirubin level in the blood. Ranges from 0 to 1 mg/dL. Higher values can suggest impaired liver function.
  • Total Proteins: The total amount of proteins in the blood. Ranges from 2 to 8 g/dL. Abnormal levels may be associated with liver issues.
  • Albumin: The level of albumin in the blood. Ranges from 1 to 6 g/dL. Deviations from the normal range may indicate liver disorders.
  • Albumin and Globulin Ratio: The ratio of albumin to globulin in the blood. Ranges vary. Abnormal ratios may be linked to liver conditions.
  • Alamine Aminotransferase (ALT): An enzyme that may indicate liver damage. Ranges vary. Elevated values may suggest liver issues.
  • Aspartate Aminotransferase (AST): An enzyme found in the liver and other tissues. Ranges vary. Abnormal levels may indicate liver problems.
  • Alkaline Phosphatase: An enzyme that may indicate liver or bone issues. Ranges vary. Elevated values may suggest liver dysfunction.

Understanding the ranges and descriptions of these features is crucial for assessing the risk of liver disease. Here's a general guideline for risk levels:

  • Low Risk: Values within the lower range of each feature.
  • High Risk: Values at the upper range, indicating a significant risk of liver disease. For specific feature ranges:
    • Total Bilirubin: 0-3 mg/dL
    • Direct Bilirubin: 0-1 mg/dL
    • Total Proteins: 2-8 g/dL
    • Albumin: 1-6 g/dL
    • Albumin and Globulin Ratio: Varies
    • Alamine Aminotransferase (ALT): Varies
    • Aspartate Aminotransferase (AST): Varies
    • Alkaline Phosphatase: Varies
