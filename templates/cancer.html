Before we delve into cancer features, let's understand how these features play a crucial role in predicting cancer risk. Each feature provides valuable information that contributes to the overall assessment.

  • Clump Thickness: The thickness of clumps of cells. Ranges from 1 to 10.
  • Uniform Cell Size: Uniformity of cell size. Ranges from 1 to 10.
  • Uniform Cell Shape: Uniformity of cell shape. Ranges from 1 to 10.
  • Marginal Adhesion: Degree of adhesion of cells. Ranges from 1 to 10.
  • Single Epithelial Cell Size: Size of individual cells. Ranges from 1 to 10.
  • Bare Nuclei: Absence of a cell membrane. Ranges from 1 to 10.
  • Bland Chromatin: Appearance of chromatin in cell nuclei. Ranges from 1 to 10.
  • Normal Nucleoli: Appearance of nucleoli. Ranges from 1 to 10.
  • Mitoses: Frequency of mitotic activity. Ranges from 1 to 10.

Understanding the ranges and descriptions of these features is crucial for assessing cancer risk. Here's a general guideline for risk levels:

  • Low Risk: Values within the lower range of each feature.
  • High Risk: Values at the upper range, indicating a significant risk of cancer. For specific feature ranges:
